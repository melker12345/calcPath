# Martin's axiom

Martin's axiom (MA) is a forcing axiom asserting that for ccc (countable chain condition) partial orders, one can find filters that are generic for any "small" (less than continuum many) collection of dense sets.

It is consistent with the continuum being arbitrarily large, and it implies many of the combinatorial and regularity consequences that were once associated with the continuum hypothesis, while allowing 2^ℵ₀ to be much larger than ℵ₁.

MA is one of the most useful "additional axioms" beyond ZFC for settling questions about the real line, cardinal invariants, and infinitary combinatorics.

## The Statement

<!-- section: statement-ma -->

MA(κ) (for a cardinal κ): If P is a ccc poset and D_α (α < κ) are dense subsets of P, then there exists a filter G ⊆ P such that G ∩ D_α ≠ ∅ for all α < κ.

Martin's axiom (MA): MA(κ) holds for every κ < 2^ℵ₀.

In other words, for ccc posets you can meet < continuum many dense sets simultaneously with a single filter.

**ELI5**

- A ccc poset is one in which you cannot find uncountably many pairwise incompatible conditions (a "wide" but "shallow" tree of possibilities).
- MA says that if you have fewer than continuum many "requirements" (dense sets) for such a poset, then the universe already contains a filter that satisfies all of them at once.
- This is a weak form of "genericity" that stops short of full choice or full forcing axioms.

**Worked Example:**

The poset for adding a Cohen real is ccc. The dense sets "the generic real differs from the α-th ground-model real at some coordinate" (one for each α < 2^ℵ₀) are dense. MA says there is a single real that differs from all ground-model reals of size < continuum; i.e., the covering number of the meager ideal is the continuum.

## Consistency Strength and Models

<!-- section: statement-ma -->

MA + ¬CH is consistent relative to ZFC. In fact, starting from a model with a large enough cardinal (or just ZFC if one is willing to make the continuum a weakly compact in an inner model, but usually one uses an inaccessible or just iterates), one can force MA + 2^ℵ₀ = λ for any regular λ ≥ ℵ₂ with uncountable cofinality by a finite-support ccc iteration of length λ that book-keeps all possible ccc posets and all possible collections of < λ dense sets.

The iteration itself remains ccc, so cardinals are preserved and the continuum becomes λ while MA holds in the extension.

MA is therefore a relatively "mild" additional axiom; it does not increase consistency strength beyond ZFC.

**ELI5**

- You can start with a universe in which the continuum is small or large, and then carefully add generics for all the ccc posets you care about, in a book-kept iteration, so that at the end the continuum is whatever size you want and every "small" collection of dense sets for ccc posets has already been met by a filter that was added along the way.
- No large cardinals are needed for the consistency of MA + ¬CH; ZFC alone suffices.

**Worked Example:**

To obtain MA + 2^ℵ₀ = ℵ₂, iterate all ccc posets of size ℵ₁ with finite support for ℵ₂ steps, dovetailing so that every possible ccc poset in intermediate models and every possible ℵ₁-sequence of dense sets appears as a stage. The whole iteration is ccc (by a delta-system argument on the supports), preserves ℵ₁ and ℵ₂, makes the continuum ℵ₂, and forces MA(ℵ₁), hence MA.

## Combinatorial Consequences

<!-- section: consequences-of-ma -->

MA has many pleasant consequences that "look like" consequences of CH but without forcing the continuum to be small:
- The union of < 2^ℵ₀ many meager sets is meager (additivity of the meager ideal = continuum).
- The union of < 2^ℵ₀ many null sets has measure zero (additivity of the null ideal = continuum).
- There are no Suslin trees.
- Every ccc poset of size < continuum has a filter meeting all dense sets of size < continuum (by definition).
- The real line cannot be the union of < continuum many countable sets in certain senses, etc.

In cardinal invariants of the continuum, MA implies that many invariants (add, cov, non, cof for both meager and null ideals) equal the continuum.

**ELI5**

- MA makes "< continuum" a "small" cardinal in the combinatorial sense: you cannot cover the reals with fewer than continuum many "small" (meager or null) sets.
- It also kills many "pathological" objects (Suslin trees) that can exist under CH or in L.

**Worked Example:**

Suppose {N_α | α < κ} with κ < 2^ℵ₀ are nowhere dense. To show their union is nowhere dense, consider the ccc poset of nonempty open intervals with rational endpoints, ordered by inclusion. The dense sets "the interval is disjoint from N_α" are dense for each α. MA gives a filter (i.e., a decreasing sequence of intervals) whose intersection point lies in no N_α and is in every basic open set we care about, showing the union is not dense in any interval.

## Relation to Other Axioms

<!-- section: relation-to-other-axioms -->

MA is strictly between ZFC and CH in strength for many purposes:
- MA + CH is consistent (in fact MA(ℵ₁) + CH is just CH plus a weak forcing axiom).
- MA + ¬CH is consistent and implies many consequences that CH also implies, but also some that CH contradicts (e.g., the non-existence of Suslin trees is compatible with both, but MA makes the additivity of ideals = continuum while CH makes it ℵ₁).

Stronger forcing axioms:
- The Proper Forcing Axiom (PFA) is the analogue of MA for all *proper* posets (a much larger class than ccc). PFA implies MA + 2^ℵ₀ = ℵ₂ and many structural statements about ω₁ (no square, every stationary set reflects or is killed in a controlled way, etc.).
- Martin's Maximum (MM) is the strongest reasonable forcing axiom (for stationary-set-preserving posets) and also implies 2^ℵ₀ = ℵ₂.

MA is implied by PFA and MM.

**ELI5**

- MA is the "ccc version" of forcing axioms: it only talks about the 'tame' ccc posets.
- PFA and MM talk about much wilder posets and therefore have much stronger consequences (in particular they pin the continuum exactly at ℵ₂).

**Worked Example:**

PFA implies that there are no ℵ₁-Suslin trees (because the tree poset is proper) and that 2^ℵ₀ = ℵ₂ (by a proper forcing that shoots a club through a stationary set coding a collapse or by other bookkeeping). MA alone does not decide the exact value of the continuum (only that it is > ℵ₁ and regular of uncountable cofinality in the usual models).

## Philosophical Status

<!-- section: philosophical-status -->

MA is often viewed as a "maximality" or "anti-CH" principle in a weak sense:
- It says that the universe cannot avoid generic filters for ccc posets when only < continuum many dense sets are involved.
- It makes many objects "small" in the cardinal-invariant sense without forcing the actual size of the continuum to be small.
- It is compatible with a wide range of values for 2^ℵ₀ and with the existence of large cardinals.

In the context of the "set theory of the real line," MA (and stronger forcing axioms) are competing with CH and with large-cardinal + determinacy approaches as candidates for "what the continuum is like."

**ELI5**

- Under CH the continuum is small, so many things collapse to ℵ₁-sized combinatorics.
- Under MA the continuum may be large, but you still get "ℵ₁-like" or "small" behavior for anything of size < continuum, because the forcing axioms guarantee generic filters for the relevant posets.
- It is a way of saying "the continuum is large, but combinatorially it still behaves as if it were the 'next' cardinal after ℵ₁ in many respects."

**Worked Example:**

Under CH there can be a Suslin tree (an ℵ₁-tree with no uncountable chains or antichains). MA kills all such trees because the tree poset is ccc and one can take the dense sets that "kill a particular node on level α" for each α < 2^ℵ₀. A filter meeting all of them would give an uncountable antichain, contradicting that it is a Suslin tree. Hence MA ⇒ "there are no Suslin trees."

## Common Mistakes

- **Thinking MA implies CH or ¬CH**: MA is compatible with both (though the interesting models usually take MA + ¬CH).
- **Confusing MA with full AC or with the existence of generics for arbitrary posets**: MA only concerns ccc posets and only < continuum many dense sets.
- **Assuming MA is a theorem of ZFC**: It is not; it is independent (consistent with ZFC + CH and with ZFC + ¬CH + large continuum).
- **Forgetting that MA has no extra consistency strength**: Unlike large cardinals, MA does not prove Con(ZFC + stronger statements); it is a purely combinatorial/ forcing axiom whose consistency is equiconsistent with ZFC.
- **Believing that MA decides the exact value of the continuum**: It forces the continuum to be regular and > ℵ₁, but the exact value (ℵ₂, ℵ₃, ℵ_{ω+1}, …) can vary in different models of MA. Stronger axioms (PFA, MM) pin it at ℵ₂.
