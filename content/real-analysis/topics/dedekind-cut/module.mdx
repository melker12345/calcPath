# Dedekind cut

A Dedekind cut is a way to define a real number directly as a "cut" in the ordered set of rationals. It was Richard Dedekind's elegant solution to the problem of filling the gaps in $\mathbb{Q}$ without appealing to decimal expansions or limits.

Cuts give an explicit construction of $\mathbb{R}$ that makes the order and the least-upper-bound property almost immediate from the definition.

## Definition of a Cut

<!-- section: definition -->

A Dedekind cut is a subset $A \subset \mathbb{Q}$ such that:

- $A$ is nonempty and not all of $\mathbb{Q}$;
- if $p \in A$ and $q < p$ (q rational) then $q \in A$ (downward closed);
- $A$ contains no greatest element.

The real number corresponding to the cut is the "position" of the cut. We often write the cut as the pair $(A, \mathbb{Q}\setminus A)$.

**ELI5**

- Picture all rationals lined up. You take a pair of scissors and cut the line into everything on the left (A) and everything on the right. The cut location is the real, whether or not a rational sits exactly at the blades.
- Downward closed + no max means the scissors never land on a rational "last leftmost" point; the cut can sit in a gap.

**Worked Example:**

The cut for zero: $A = \{ q \in \mathbb{Q} \mid q < 0 \}$. It is downward closed, nonempty, proper, and has no largest element (if q<0 then (q+0)/2 <0 and larger).

## Arithmetic Operations on Cuts

<!-- section: operations -->

For cuts $A$ and $B$:

- Sum: $A + B = \{ a + b \mid a \in A, b \in B \}$ (the lower set of the sum).
- For positive cuts, product can be defined via positives; general product uses signs.
- Order: $A \le B$ iff $A \subseteq B$.

These operations turn the set of all cuts into an ordered field. The embedding of a rational r is the cut of all q < r.

**ELI5**

- Adding cuts: slide the two cuts by each other's amounts; the combined left-set is everything reachable by adding a left from each.
- The inclusion order matches "to the left of".

**Worked Example:**

The cut for 1 is all q <1. The cut for √2 (positive) has lower set all q with q<0 or q²<2. Their sum cut corresponds to 1+√2; one verifies the resulting lower set satisfies the cut axioms.

## Why Cuts Give a Complete Ordered Field

<!-- section: why-cuts-work -->

Given any nonempty collection of cuts that is bounded above (by another cut), the union of all the lower sets in the collection is again a cut, and it is the least upper bound of the collection.

This immediately yields the LUB property inside the cuts. One then proves the other field axioms hold, that the cuts contain an isomorphic copy of $\mathbb{Q}$, and that the resulting object is unique up to isomorphism (any two complete ordered fields are isomorphic).

**ELI5**

- The sup of a bunch of cuts is simply "the rightmost cut you can form by taking the union of everything that was on the left for at least one of them."
- Because union preserves the downward-closed and no-max properties, the sup is again a legitimate cut.

**Worked Example:**

Let C be the collection of all cuts A such that A is the cut for some rational q with q² < 2. Then ∪_{A in C} A is exactly the Dedekind cut for √2, which is the sup of the collection inside the reals constructed from cuts.

## Common Mistakes

- **Forgetting the 'no greatest element' clause**: Without it every rational would correspond to two cuts (one including the rational on the left, one on the right), breaking the bijection with reals.
- **Thinking cuts are subsets of reals**: Cuts partition the rationals; the reals are the cuts.
- **Confusing a cut with its 'value'**: Until the isomorphism is proved one must manipulate sets of rationals, not assume the cut 'is' a number you already know.
- **Neglecting to check that sum/product of cuts is again a cut**: The verification that A+B is downward closed and has no max is part of the construction.
- **Assuming the construction is the only possible one**: Cauchy sequences give an equivalent but different-looking model.
