# Complex plane

Plotting complex numbers as points in the plane turns algebra into geometry and reveals multiplication as rotation+scaling.

## The Complex Plane

<!-- section: complex-plane -->

Plot z = a + bi as the point (a, b) in the Cartesian plane. The horizontal axis is the real axis; vertical is the imaginary axis.

- |z| is the distance from origin to the point.
- Argument arg(z) is the angle from positive real axis to the ray from origin through z (principal value in (-π, π]).

Addition is parallelogram law (vector sum). Multiplication by a fixed complex number is a similarity transformation (scale + rotate).

**ELI5**

- The complex plane turns algebra into geometry: every complex number is a point or an arrow from the origin. Pure imaginaries live on the vertical axis, reals on the horizontal.

**Worked Example:**

**Plot  -1 + 2i , compute its modulus and principal argument**

1. Point (-1, 2) in quadrant II.
2. |z| = sqrt(1 + 4) = √5.
3. arg = π - arctan(2/1) ≈ 3.1416 - 1.107 = 2.034 rad (or leave as π - arctan 2).

## Common Mistakes

- **Principal argument range**: arg usually taken in (-π, π]; using (0,2π] or other intervals can cause sign errors in polar form.
- **Plotting imag as x**: Real is horizontal x, imag vertical y. 3-2i is (3,-2) not (-2,3).
- **arg of negative real**: arg(-1) = π (or -π boundary), not 0.
- **Modulus vs argument mixup**: |z| is length (always ≥0), arg is angle.
- **Forgetting conjugate is reflection over real axis**: a-bi is (a,-b).
- **Vector addition vs complex**: Same, but remember i direction is perpendicular.
