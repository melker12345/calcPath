# Unit circle

The unit circle is the dictionary for all trig values. Memorizing the key angles gives exact answers without calculator.

## The Unit Circle

<!-- section: unit-circle -->

The **unit circle** is the circle of radius 1 centered at origin. Points are (cos θ, sin θ) where θ is the arc length (radians) from positive x-axis.

Common angles and exact values:

| θ (radians) | θ (degrees) | sin θ     | cos θ     | tan θ     |
|-------------|-------------|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| 0           | 0°          | 0         | 1         | 0         |
| π/6         | 30°         | 1/2       | √3/2      | 1/√3      |
| π/4         | 45°         | √2/2      | √2/2      | 1         |
| π/3         | 60°         | √3/2      | 1/2       | √3        |
| π/2         | 90°         | 1         | 0         | undef     |
| π           | 180°        | 0         | -1        | 0         |
| 3π/2        | 270°        | -1        | 0         | undef     |
| 2π          | 360°        | 0         | 1         | 0         |

Memorizing these (and signs by quadrant) lets you evaluate trig functions exactly without calculator.

**ELI5**

- The unit circle is a "dictionary" that translates any angle directly into its sin and cos by walking the distance around the circle and reading the coordinates. Radians make the numbers match the actual arc length.

**Worked Example:**

**Exact value of cos(5π/3)**

1. 5π/3 = 300° is in quadrant IV (reference angle 60°).
2. cos positive in IV: cos(60°)=1/2.
3. Thus cos(5π/3) = +1/2.

## Common Mistakes

- **Radians vs degrees**: Calculators and most advanced math use radians by default. sin(30) in radians is not 0.5; always check mode.
- **Reference angle signs**: Forgetting to apply the correct sign for sine/cosine/tangent in each quadrant leads to wrong signs.
- **Unit circle memorization gaps**: Not knowing the 30-45-60 exact values forces calculator use and loses exact answers on exams.
- **Forgetting undef at π/2 +kπ for tan/sec/csc/cot**: Those points have cos or sin zero.
- **θ in degrees on unit circle table**: The table is for radians; convert if needed.
- **Negative angles**: Equivalent to clockwise; use even/odd or add 2π k to positive.
