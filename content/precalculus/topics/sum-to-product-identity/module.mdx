# Sum-to-product identity

These identities collapse sums of sines or cosines into products. Magic for solving equations and simplifying waves (beats).

## Sum-to-Product and Product-to-Sum Identities

<!-- section: sum-to-product -->

Sum-to-product:

$\sin A + \sin B = 2 \sin(\frac{A+B}{2}) \cos(\frac{A-B}{2})$

$\sin A - \sin B = 2 \cos(\frac{A+B}{2}) \sin(\frac{A-B}{2})$

$\cos A + \cos B = 2 \cos(\frac{A+B}{2}) \cos(\frac{A-B}{2})$

$\cos A - \cos B = -2 \sin(\frac{A+B}{2}) \sin(\frac{A-B}{2})$

These turn sums into products, helpful for solving equations and simplifying.

**ELI5**

- Instead of adding two sine waves of different phases, the identity collapses them into a single sine or cosine times a slow envelope — the math behind "beats" in sound.

**Worked Example:**

**Evaluate sin 75° + sin 15° exactly**

1. A=75, B=15: 2 sin(45) cos(30) = 2 * (√2/2) * (√3/2) = √2 * √3 / 2 = √6 / 2.

## Common Mistakes

- **Sum-to-product arithmetic**: (A+B)/2 and (A-B)/2 must be computed carefully; mixing up which is which ruins the identity application.
- **Sign in cos A - cos B**: Has the minus in front of the sin sin term.
- **Forgetting product-to-sum the other way**: Sometimes needed too.
- **Using when A or B make arg undefined**: Check domains.
- **Calculator for exact**: Loses the simplified radical.
- **Confusing with double angle**: Similar looking but different (A=B case recovers double).
