# Trigonometric identity

Identities are the algebra rules for trig. They let us rewrite expressions to simplify, solve, or integrate.

## Basic Trigonometric Identities

<!-- section: basic-identities -->

Fundamental identities:

1. Pythagorean: $\sin^2 \theta + \cos^2 \theta = 1$
   - Also: $1 + \tan^2 \theta = \sec^2 \theta$, $1 + \cot^2 \theta = \csc^2 \theta$

2. Reciprocal: $\csc \theta = 1/\sin$, etc.

3. Quotient: $\tan \theta = \sin/\cos$, $\cot = \cos/\sin$

4. Even/odd: cos even, sin and tan odd.

These allow simplification and are the starting point for proving more complex identities.

**ELI5**

- Identities are "always-true trig facts" you can use like algebra rules to rewrite messy expressions into simpler ones or to solve equations.

**Worked Example:**

**Prove that $\frac{\sin \theta}{\tan \theta} = \cos \theta$ (for tan defined)**

1. Right side: cos θ.
2. Left: sin θ / (sin θ / cos θ) = sin θ * (cos θ / sin θ) = cos θ.
3. Both sides equal, identity holds.

## Common Mistakes

- **Identity direction**: sin² + cos² =1 can be used to find one from the other, but you must take ± square root and choose sign from quadrant info.
- **Forgetting to state domain restrictions**: Identities hold where the expressions are defined (e.g. tan defined).
- **Using = when proving**: Start with one side, transform with = steps to other side; don't assume the thing to prove in middle.
- **Confusing identities with equations to solve**: Identities are true for all (in domain); equations are true for specific values.
- **Sign errors in even/odd**: cos even, sin tan odd.
- **Quotient vs reciprocal**: tan = sin/cos (quotient), csc=1/sin (reciprocal).
