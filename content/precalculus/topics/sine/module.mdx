# Sine

Sine is the fundamental periodic function, giving the vertical position as you travel around the unit circle.

## Sine

<!-- section: sine -->

The **sine** function sin θ gives the y-coordinate of the point on the unit circle reached by traveling an arc length θ (in radians) counterclockwise from (1,0).

Properties: odd function (sin(-θ) = -sin θ), range [-1,1], period 2π, zeros at integer multiples of π.

Sine is the "vertical" component in circular motion and the imaginary part of e^{iθ}.

**ELI5**

- Sine tells you "how high or low you are" as you walk around a circle at constant speed. It starts at 0, goes up to 1 at 90°, back to 0 at 180°, down to -1, and repeats.

**Worked Example:**

**Graph key points of y = sin x over one period**

At x=0: sin0=0

x=π/2: 1

x=π: 0

x=3π/2: -1

x=2π: 0

The curve is a smooth wave oscillating between -1 and 1 with wavelength 2π.

## Common Mistakes

- **Radians vs degrees**: Calculators and most advanced math use radians by default. sin(30) in radians is not 0.5; always check mode.
- **Sign errors in quadrants**: sin positive in QI and QII, negative in QIII QIV.
- **Period of sin vs tan**: sin period 2π; tan π.
- **Forgetting sin is odd**: sin(-θ) = -sin θ; useful for symmetry.
- **Zeros at kπ not k π/2**: At π/2 sin=1, not zero.
- **Range always [-1,1]**: No matter the angle, |sin| ≤1.
