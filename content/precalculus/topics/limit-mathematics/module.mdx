# Limit (mathematics)

The limit is the central idea that makes calculus work: the value the function approaches as input approaches a point (even if the function is not defined or different there).

## Limits (Introduction)

<!-- section: limits-intro -->

The **limit** concept is the foundation of calculus. We say lim_{x→a} f(x) = L if f(x) can be made arbitrarily close to L by making x sufficiently close to a (x may or may not equal a).

Notation and one-sided limits, infinite limits, and limits at infinity are introduced here.

Limits let us define continuity (lim = f(a)), derivatives (instantaneous rate), and integrals (accumulated area) rigorously.

Many limits are evaluated by direct substitution, factoring/canceling, rationalizing, or using known special limits such as lim (sin x)/x =1 as x→0.

**ELI5**

- The limit asks: "If I sneak up on this x-value from nearby (without necessarily landing on it), what value does the function settle toward?" It describes the intended height even if there's a hole or jump right at the point.

**Worked Example:**

**Evaluate lim_{x→2} (x² - 4)/(x - 2)**

1. Direct plug x=2 gives 0/0 indeterminate.
2. Factor numerator: (x-2)(x+2) / (x-2) , for x≠2.
3. Cancels to x+2 when x≠2.
4. Therefore the limit (behavior near 2) is 2 + 2 = 4.

## Common Mistakes

- **Limit vs function value**: The limit can exist even when f(a) is undefined or different (removable discontinuity). Always distinguish lim x→a f(x) from f(a).
- **One-sided limits**: For limits at endpoints or jumps, left and right must agree; otherwise the two-sided limit does not exist.
- **Indeterminate forms**: 0/0 or ∞/∞ require algebraic manipulation or other techniques (L'Hôpital later); do not conclude "does not exist" immediately.
- **Forgetting to check one-sided at discontinuities**: Jump discontinuities have different left/right limits.
- **Plugging in when there is a hole**: Always simplify first if possible.
- **Limits at infinity vs at a point**: Different techniques (divide by highest power for ∞).
