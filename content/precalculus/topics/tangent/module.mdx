# Tangent

Tangent is the slope of the line from origin to the unit circle point. It has period π and vertical asymptotes.

## Tangent

<!-- section: tangent -->

**Tangent** tan θ = sin θ / cos θ = opposite/adjacent.

It has period π, range all reals, vertical asymptotes at odd multiples of π/2, zeros at integer multiples of π.

The tangent function is useful for slopes (rise over run) and appears in many trig substitution integrals.

**ELI5**

- Tangent is the slope of the line from origin to the point on the circle. As you approach 90° from left the slope shoots to +∞; just after 90° it comes from -∞.

**Worked Example:**

**Finding tan of 135°**

1. 135° = 180° - 45°, reference 45°.
2. In quadrant II sin positive, cos negative.
3. sin135= sin45=√2/2, cos135= -√2/2.
4. tan = (√2/2) / (-√2/2) = -1.

## Common Mistakes

- **Radians vs degrees**: Calculators and most advanced math use radians by default. sin(30) in radians is not 0.5; always check mode.
- **Undefined tangent**: tan and sec are undefined at odd multiples of π/2; students sometimes plot them or solve equations as if defined there.
- **Period π not 2π**: tan repeats every 180°, not 360°.
- **Sign in quadrants**: tan positive QI QIII, negative QII QIV.
- **tan = opp/adj only for acute**: For general angles use unit circle or reference with signs.
- **Confusing with arctan range**: arctan range (-π/2,π/2) not the same as tan's behavior.
