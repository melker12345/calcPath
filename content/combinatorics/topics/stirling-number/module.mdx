# Stirling number

Stirling numbers come in two flavors and are the "change of basis" coefficients between the monomial basis and the falling/rising factorial bases. Combinatorially they count set partitions (second kind) and permutations by number of cycles (first kind).

They are fundamental in enumerative combinatorics and appear in many explicit formulas.

## Stirling Numbers of the Second Kind

<!-- section: stirling-second -->

S(n,k) = number of ways to partition an n-element set into exactly k non-empty *unlabeled* subsets.

The total Bell number B_n = sum_k S(n,k) counts all partitions with any number of blocks.

**ELI5**

- S(4,2)=7: the ways to split 4 people into two non-empty teams where the teams themselves are not distinguished ( { {1,2,3},{4} } is one partition).
- If the teams were labeled "team A and team B" you would multiply by k!.

**Worked Example:**

S(3,2) = 3: for {1,2,3} the partitions into 2 blocks are {{1,2}{3}}, {{1,3}{2}}, {{1}{2,3}}.

## Stirling Numbers of the First Kind

<!-- section: stirling-first -->

The unsigned Stirling numbers of the first kind c(n,k) (or |s(n,k)|) count the number of permutations of n elements with exactly k cycles.

The signed versions s(n,k) = (−1)^{n-k} c(n,k) appear in the change-of-basis (x)_n = sum s(n,k) x^k for the falling factorial.

**ELI5**

- A permutation can be decomposed into disjoint cycles. c(n,k) is "how many permutations have exactly k cycles in their cycle decomposition".
- For n=3: identity has 3 cycles (fixed points), the 3 transpositions have 2 cycles each (one 2-cycle + one fixed), the two 3-cycles have 1 cycle.

**Worked Example:**

c(4,2)=11. There are 11 permutations of [4] with exactly 2 cycles.

## Relation to Bell Numbers and Other Objects

<!-- section: bell-relation -->

B_n = sum_k S(n,k) is the nth Bell number.

Also S(n,k) = 1/k! sum_{j=0}^k (−1)^{k-j} \binom{k}{j} j^n  (inclusion-exclusion for surjections divided by k!).

The EGF for the column S(n,k) fixed k is (e^x − 1)^k / k! .

**ELI5**

- Bell numbers are the "total partitions" row sums of the Stirling triangle of the second kind.
- The explicit sum formula comes from inclusion-exclusion on the number of functions onto exactly a labeled k-set, then divide by k! because blocks unlabeled.

**Worked Example:**

B_4 = 15.

From the formula or table: 1 + 7 + 6 + 1 = 15.

## Common Mistakes

- **Confusing first and second kind**: Second kind = set partitions (blocks), first kind = cycle counts in permutations.
- **Forgetting that blocks/subsets are unlabeled**: S(n,k) already treats the k subsets as indistinguishable; k! S(n,k) = number of surjections.
- **Sign errors with signed Stirling first**: The signed ones alternate so that the polynomial identity holds with falling factorials.
- **Using S(n,k) when k > n or k=0 (except n=0)**: S(n,0)=0 for n>0, S(0,0)=1.
- **Mixing Bell with Stirling**: Bell is the sum; individual S(n,k) are the "exactly k blocks" numbers.
