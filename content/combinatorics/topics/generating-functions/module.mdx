# Generating Functions & Recurrences

Ordinary and exponential generating functions, and recurrence relations for encoding and solving counting problems.

A generating function packages an entire infinite sequence into a single formal power series. By manipulating the series algebraically we can often extract the nth coefficient — i.e., solve the original counting problem — without listing terms.

Ordinary generating functions (OGFs) are the workhorse for unlabeled or unordered structures; they turn addition of sizes into multiplication of series.

## Ordinary Generating Functions

<!-- section: ordinary-generating -->

For a sequence a_0, a_1, a_2, … the ordinary generating function is

$$A(x) = \sum_{n=0}^\infty a_n x^n = a_0 + a_1 x + a_2 x^2 + \cdots$$

The exponent marks the parameter we care about (usually size n); the coefficient a_n is the answer to the counting question for that n.

**ELI5**

- Imagine a vending machine that for each possible "size" n drops out a_n different candy combinations. The generating function is the "master list" written as a polynomial you can add and multiply.
- The beauty is that algebraic operations on the master list correspond to combining choices.

**Worked Example:**

Number of ways to make n cents using only 1-cent coins: a_n = 1 for all n.

GF: 1 + x + x^2 + x^3 + … = 1/(1 − x).

## Operations and Their Combinatorial Meaning

<!-- section: operations-on-gfs -->

- Sum A(x) + B(x): disjoint union of two classes.
- Product A(x)B(x): ordered pairs (or sequences of two independent structures) whose sizes add.
- 1/(1 − A(x)): any number (including zero) of copies of structures from A, sizes adding (compositions).

These turn recursive or compositional counting into algebra.

**ELI5**

- Multiplying two GFs is like "first choose a red item of size k (a_k ways) and independently a blue item of size n-k (b ways)"; the coeff sums all splits.
- The geometric series 1/(1-x) = sum x^n corresponds to "any number of 1-cent coins."

**Worked Example:**

Unrestricted number of identical apples and identical oranges.

GF per fruit type: 1 + x + x^2 + … = 1/(1-x).

Combined: [1/(1-x)]^2 = sum (n+1) x^n.

Thus there are n+1 ways to have total n fruits (0 to n apples, rest oranges).

## Solving Classic Counting Problems

<!-- section: solving-counting-problems -->

Many classic problems become routine once the GF is written:

- Integer partitions → product over part sizes 1/(1-x^k)
- Compositions with restricted part sizes → 1 / (1 − (x + x^2 + … + x^m))
- Tilings, paths, matchings → often lead to linear recurrences whose characteristic equation appears in the denominator.

After writing the GF one can expand, use partial fractions, or extract [x^n] via recurrence or other tools.

**ELI5**

- "How many ways to pay exactly n cents with standard US coins?" Write the product of geometric series for each denomination; the coefficient of x^n is the answer.
- The algebra does the heavy casework for you.

**Worked Example:**

Number of ways to tile a 2 × n board with dominoes (each covers two squares).

Let f_n be the number. A tiling either ends with a vertical domino (then f_{n-1} before) or two horizontal (f_{n-2} before).

Thus f_n = f_{n-1} + f_{n-2}, f_0=1, f_1=1 → Fibonacci.

The GF F(x) = 1 / (1 − x − x^2) encodes all f_n.

## Common Mistakes

- **Confusing ordinary with exponential generating functions**: OGFs for unlabeled/unordered; EGFs (with /n!) for labeled or ordered structures like permutations.
- **Treating the series as convergent real functions too early**: In combinatorics we usually work formally; analytic questions come later.
- **Forgetting the constant term**: The empty structure almost always contributes the 1 (x^0 term).
- **Misidentifying what the exponent represents**: Make sure x marks exactly the parameter you want to count (size, weight, etc.).
- **Writing the wrong factor for "unlimited supply"**: Unlimited identical items of one type → 1/(1-x^k) not (1+x^k).

Exponential generating functions (EGFs) are the natural tool when the objects being counted are *labeled* — distinct people, numbered positions, or distinguishable atoms.

The division by n! in the definition cancels the n! ways to label an n-element structure, yielding clean algebraic expressions for sets, sequences, and cycles of labeled components.

## Definition of EGFs

<!-- section: definition-egf -->

The exponential generating function for the sequence a_n (number of structures on an n-element labeled set) is

$$A(x) = \sum_{n \ge 0} \frac{a_n}{n!} x^n.$$

To recover the count: a_n = n! [x^n] A(x).

**ELI5**

- Because labels can be permuted in n! ways, we "pre-divide" by n! so that the series coefficients directly reflect the "shape" count.
- For permutations of [n] there are n! of them; their EGF simplifies to sum x^n = 1/(1-x) — beautiful.

**Worked Example:**

Number of ways to choose a subset of [n] and then order it: a_n = n! (any permutation of any subset? Wait no.

For all permutations: a_n = n!, EGF = sum n! (x^n / n!) = sum x^n = 1/(1-x).

## Labeled Structures and Set Constructions

<!-- section: labeled-structures -->

For labeled objects the basic building blocks translate to:

- A single labeled atom: x
- A non-empty set of labeled atoms: e^x − 1
- A sequence of labeled structures: 1 / (1 − A(x))
- A set of labeled structures: exp(A(x))

These are far cleaner than the corresponding ordinary generating functions for the same labeled counts.

**ELI5**

- "A set of cycles" (permutations) has EGF exp( − ln(1−x) ) = 1/(1−x).
- "Set partitions" : the blocks are non-empty sets, so EGF exp( e^x − 1 ).

**Worked Example:**

Number of set partitions of an n-set (Bell number B_n).

The EGF is exp(e^x − 1).

B_4 = 15. Indeed [x^4]/4! of the series gives the count after *4!.

## Relation to Ordinary Generating Functions

<!-- section: relation-to-ogf -->

When structures are unlabeled, OGFs are preferred. When labels matter (or when we count permutations, functions, mappings), EGFs usually produce simpler closed forms and composition rules that mirror the combinatorial constructions directly.

Many species in combinatorial enumeration theory are defined via their EGFs.

**ELI5**

- If the people are distinguishable, EGFs "factor out" the labeling so the algebra sees only the grouping.
- Converting between OGF and EGF is possible via Borel or other transforms but usually we choose the right one from the start.

**Worked Example:**

Derangements (permutations with no fixed points): d(n) ≈ n!/e.

EGF: e^{−x} / (1 − x).

Then d(n) = n! [x^n] (e^{-x}/(1-x)).

For n=4: 9.

## Common Mistakes

- **Forgetting the n! multiplier when extracting counts from EGF**: a_n = n! [x^n] A(x), not just the coefficient.
- **Using EGF for unlabeled objects like integer partitions**: The /n! would be meaningless or wrong; use OGF instead.
- **Confusing the constructions**: "set of" is exp, "sequence of" is 1/(1-A); swapping them is a common error.
- **Treating EGFs as probability generating functions**: They are formal counting tools; the /n! is combinatorial, not probabilistic.
- **Ignoring the empty structure**: The constant term is almost always 1 for the empty labeled set.

A recurrence relation defines a sequence by expressing later terms in terms of earlier ones. Many combinatorial counts — tiling numbers, tree counts, path counts, partition numbers — naturally satisfy simple recurrences that can be solved for closed forms or generating functions.

Solving recurrences is the bridge between recursive thinking and explicit formulas.

## Linear Recurrences with Constant Coefficients

<!-- section: linear-recurrences -->

A recurrence is linear homogeneous constant-coefficient of order d if a_n = c_1 a_{n-1} + … + c_d a_{n-d}.

The characteristic equation r^d − c_1 r^{d-1} − … − c_d = 0 determines the form of the solution: linear combination of r_i^n (or n^k r^n for repeated roots).

Initial conditions fix the constants.

**ELI5**

- The Fibonacci rule "each term is sum of previous two" is a linear recurrence. Its solutions are mixtures of the golden-ratio powers.
- Once you have the closed form you can compute the 1000th term without iterating 1000 steps.

**Worked Example:**

a_n = 2 a_{n-1} + a_{n-2}, a_0=0, a_1=1.

Char eq r^2 -2r -1=0, roots 1±√2.

General: A (1+√2)^n + B (1-√2)^n.

Fit initials → explicit Binet-like form.

## Combinatorial Recurrences

<!-- section: combinatorial-recurrences -->

Many counting problems have recurrences by considering the "last piece" or "root structure".

Classic: number of binary trees, triangulations, Dyck paths, tilings, matchings all obey Catalan or Fibonacci-style recurrences obtained by case analysis on a distinguished element.

**ELI5**

- A valid parentheses string of length 2n either is () + valid of 2(n-1), or ( valid of 2k ) + valid of 2(n-1-k).
- The case split directly writes the recurrence (or the GF equation).

**Worked Example:**

Let a_n = number of ways to climb n stairs taking 1 or 2 at a time.

a_n = a_{n-1} (last step 1) + a_{n-2} (last two steps).

a_0=1, a_1=1 → Fibonacci. a_5 = 8.

## Solving Techniques and Generating Functions

<!-- section: solving-techniques -->

Besides the characteristic-root method, one can use generating functions: multiply the recurrence by x^n, sum, solve for the GF, then expand or partial-fraction.

For non-linear or combinatorial recurrences the GF often yields algebraic or functional equations whose solution gives the count via coefficient extraction (or Lagrange inversion for tree-like cases).

**ELI5**

- The recurrence becomes an equation for the unknown GF; algebra solves for the GF; then you read off coefficients.
- For Catalan numbers the GF C(x) satisfies C = 1 + x C^2 , solved by quadratic formula, then binomial series gives the formula.

**Worked Example:**

For the tiling/ stair recurrence a_n = a_{n-1} + a_{n-2}.

Multiply by x^n sum n≥2: A(x) - a0 -a1 x = x (A -a0) + x^2 A .

Solve for A(x) = (1) / (1 - x - x^2) after plugging initials.

The series coefficients are the Fibonacci numbers.

## Common Mistakes

- **Forgetting initial conditions**: The recurrence describes many sequences; without a_0 … a_{d-1} you cannot determine a unique sequence.
- **Assuming all recurrences are linear constant coeff**: Many natural ones (e.g. involving min or max, or convolutions) are not; they may still have GFs.
- **Wrong characteristic equation**: Sign errors when moving terms: a_n - c1 a_{n-1} … =0.
- **Ignoring multiplicity of roots**: Repeated roots require the extra n, n^2, … polynomial factors.
- **Computing only the recurrence without verifying small values**: Always compute the first few terms by hand to check the recurrence setup.
