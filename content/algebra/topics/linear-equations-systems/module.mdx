# Linear Equations & Systems

Solving linear equations and systems of linear equations in one or more variables.

A linear equation in one variable is the simplest nontrivial equation type in algebra. It can always be written as ax + b = 0 with a ≠ 0, and it has exactly one solution.

Linear equations model countless real-world situations where change happens at a constant rate: distance at constant speed, cost with a fixed fee plus per-unit charge, temperature conversions, and many more.

## Standard Form and Equivalent Forms

<!-- section: standard-form -->

Any linear equation ax + b = 0 can be rearranged into several useful forms:

- Standard form: ax + by = c (for two variables).
- Slope-intercept: y = mx + b.
- Point-slope: y − y₁ = m(x − x₁).

All represent the same straight-line relationship.

**ELI5**

- Standard form is the "neat filing cabinet" version with all terms on one side.
- Slope-intercept is the "ready to graph" version that tells you the tilt and where it crosses the y-axis.

**Worked Example:**

3x − 6 = 0 is the same as 3x = 6 and x = 2. In two variables the line 3x − y = 6 has slope 3 and y-intercept −6 when solved for y.

## Solving Linear Equations

<!-- section: solving -->

The goal is to isolate the variable using inverse operations while preserving equality.

1. Simplify both sides (distribute, combine like terms).
2. Move all terms containing the variable to one side.
3. Move constants to the other side.
4. Divide by the coefficient of the variable.

**ELI5**

- Each step peels away one layer around the variable, exactly like unwrapping a gift without tearing the prize inside.
- The balance-scale picture helps: whatever you do to one pan you must do to the other.

**Worked Example:**

Solve 4(x − 2) = 3x + 5.

Distribute: 4x − 8 = 3x + 5.

Subtract 3x: x − 8 = 5.

Add 8: x = 13.

## Slope-Intercept Form

<!-- section: slope-intercept -->

When a linear equation is solved for y, y = mx + b, m is the slope (rise over run) and b is the y-intercept (the point where the line crosses the vertical axis).

This form is ideal for graphing by hand: plot the intercept, then use the slope to find a second point.

**ELI5**

- Slope m tells how steep the line is and whether it goes up or down as you move right.
- b tells the "starting height" when x = 0.

**Worked Example:**

y = −2x + 4 has slope −2 (down 2 for every 1 right) and crosses y-axis at (0, 4). When x = 1, y = 2; when x = 2, y = 0.

## Applications of Linear Equations

<!-- section: applications -->

Linear equations arise whenever a quantity changes at a constant rate.

- Cost = fixed fee + rate × quantity
- Distance = speed × time (when speed constant)
- Temperature conversion: F = (9/5)C + 32

Translate the English carefully into symbols, solve, and interpret the number in context.

**ELI5**

- Many everyday "if this, then how much?" questions are secretly linear equations in disguise.
- Once you write the equation the algebra does the arithmetic for you.

**Worked Example:**

A taxi charges $3 to get in plus $2.50 per mile. How far can you ride for $15.50?

3 + 2.5m = 15.5 → 2.5m = 12.5 → m = 5 miles.

## Common Mistakes

- **Distributing incorrectly across parentheses**: 3(x − 2) = 3x − 6, never 3x − 2.
- **Moving a term across the equals sign without flipping its sign**: x − 4 = 7 becomes x = 7 + 4, not x = 7 − 4.
- **Dividing only one side by the coefficient**: Both sides must be divided.
- **Forgetting to check the solution**: Especially after distributing or combining terms.
- **Assuming every linear equation must be written ax + b = 0 before solving**: Any equivalent form can be solved directly.

A system of linear equations is a set of two or more equations that must be satisfied simultaneously by the same values of the variables.

The solution is the intersection point (or points) of the lines (or planes) represented by the equations. In two variables the three possibilities are one solution, no solution (parallel lines), or infinitely many solutions (same line).

## Definition and Geometric Meaning

<!-- section: definition -->

Each equation in the system graphs as a straight line. A pair (x, y) solves the system only if the point lies on every line in the system.

Consistent systems have at least one solution. Inconsistent systems have none. Dependent systems have infinitely many.

**ELI5**

- Think of two roads that cross at one town: that town is the solution.
- If the roads are parallel they never meet (no solution). If they are the exact same road, every point on the road works (infinitely many).

**Worked Example:**

The system x + y = 5 and x − y = 1 has the unique solution (3, 2) because that point lies on both lines.

## Solving by Substitution

<!-- section: substitution -->

1. Solve one equation for one variable (choose the easiest).
2. Substitute that expression into the remaining equation(s).
3. Solve the resulting equation in one variable.
4. Back-substitute to find the other variable(s).

**ELI5**

- Solve for one "mystery box" and drop the expression into the other equation like a replacement part.
- You reduce the number of unknowns step by step.

**Worked Example:**

y = 3x − 1 and 2x + y = 9.

Substitute: 2x + (3x − 1) = 9 → 5x = 10 → x = 2.

Then y = 3(2) − 1 = 5. Solution (2, 5).

## Solving by Elimination

<!-- section: elimination -->

1. Multiply one or both equations by constants so that the coefficients of one variable become opposites.
2. Add the equations; that variable cancels.
3. Solve for the remaining variable.
4. Back-substitute.

**ELI5**

- Line up the like terms and make the numbers in front of one variable cancel when you add, like removing one color of blocks from two piles.
- What remains is a simpler equation.

**Worked Example:**

3x + 2y = 7 and 5x − 2y = 1.

Add directly: 8x = 8 → x = 1.

Substitute: 3(1) + 2y = 7 → 2y = 4 → y = 2.

## Applications

<!-- section: applications -->

Systems appear whenever two conditions must be satisfied at once: "the sum is 15 and the difference is 3", "cost and revenue both depend on quantity and we want break-even", "two distances add to a total with different speeds."

Translate each English sentence into an equation, solve the system, and check that the numbers make sense together.

**ELI5**

- Two facts about the same pair of numbers give two equations. Solving finds the unique pair that satisfies both facts.
- Word problems become systems when there are two unknowns.

**Worked Example:**

The sum of two numbers is 15; their difference is 3. Let larger = x, smaller = y.

x + y = 15

x − y = 3

Add: 2x = 18 → x = 9, y = 6.

## Common Mistakes

- **Solving only one equation and forgetting the second**: The pair must work in every equation of the system.
- **Arithmetic sign errors during elimination**: A minus sign mistake when subtracting equations ruins the cancellation.
- **Not multiplying both sides of an equation when scaling**: You must multiply every term, including the constant.
- **Assuming every system has exactly one solution**: Check for parallel or identical lines (same slope and intercept).
- **Mixing up the variables after back-substitution**: Label clearly which number belongs to which variable.
