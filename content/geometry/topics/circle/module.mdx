# Circle

A circle is the set of all points in a plane that are at a fixed distance (the radius) from a given center point.

It is the most symmetric of all plane figures and appears everywhere from wheels to orbits to the definition of π.

## Definition and Basic Terminology

<!-- section: definition-terms -->

Center O, radius r (distance from center to any point on the circle), diameter d = 2r (longest chord, passing through center), circumference C = 2πr, area A = πr^{2}.

A chord is a segment joining two points on the circle. An arc is a portion of the circle between two points.

**ELI5**

- A circle is every point exactly r steps away from the center, like a perfect round fence with the center as the post.
- The diameter is the widest straight cut through the middle; twice as wide as from center to edge.

**Worked Example:**

A circle with center (0,0) and radius 5 consists of all (x,y) satisfying x^{2} + y^{2} = 25. The point (3,4) lies on it because 9+16=25. The diameter along the x-axis runs from (−5,0) to (5,0).

## Chords, Arcs, and Central Angles

<!-- section: chords-arcs -->

A central angle is formed by two radii. The measure of the arc equals the measure of the central angle. Longer chords subtend larger central angles (in the same circle).

Equal chords subtend equal arcs (and equal central angles).

**ELI5**

- The "slice" of pie from the center (central angle) tells you how big the crust arc is.
- A bigger slice has a longer crust piece and a longer straight chord across the top of the slice.

**Worked Example:**

In a circle of radius 10, central angle 60° subtends an arc of 60° (1/6 of the circumference). The chord length is 2 * 10 * sin(30°) = 10 (equilateral triangle formed by two radii and chord).

## Inscribed Angles and Angles in Semicircles

<!-- section: inscribed -->

An inscribed angle has its vertex on the circle and sides through two other points. Its measure is half the measure of the intercepted arc (or half the central angle).

A special case: an angle inscribed in a semicircle is a right angle (Thales' theorem).

**ELI5**

- An angle sitting on the edge of the circle "sees" only half as much of the arc as the center does.
- If the arc is exactly a semicircle (180°), the inscribed angle is 90° — that's why a diameter makes a right triangle with any point on the circle.

**Worked Example:**

Arc AB measures 80° (central). An inscribed angle with vertex on the remaining part of the circle intercepting arc AB measures 40°. If arc AB is 180°, the inscribed angle is 90°.

## Common Mistakes

- **Confusing central and inscribed angles**: Inscribed is half the central for the same arc.
- **Thinking the diameter is any chord through the center**: Only the one passing through center is diameter; others are just long chords.
- **Forgetting that all radii are equal**: This is what makes the circle so symmetric and allows so many isosceles triangles inside it.
- **Mixing arc measure (degrees) with arc length (units of length)**: Arc measure is angle; length is (theta/360)*2πr.
