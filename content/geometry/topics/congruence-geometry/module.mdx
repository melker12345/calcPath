# Congruence (geometry)

Two geometric figures are congruent if one can be transformed into the other by a rigid motion (isometry): translation, rotation, or reflection. They have the same size and shape; corresponding parts are equal.

Congruence is the "same" relation for figures and is central to proofs in Euclidean geometry.

## Definition via Isometries

<!-- section: definition-isometries -->

A congruence is a distance-preserving transformation (isometry) of the plane. If there exists an isometry mapping figure F1 onto F2, then F1 ≅ F2.

For triangles, this reduces to the familiar SSS, SAS, ASA, AAS, HL criteria.

**ELI5**

- Congruent figures are perfect copies; you can pick one up (without stretching or flipping in 3D if we disallow reflections sometimes) and lay it exactly on the other.
- "Same shape and same size" is the everyday phrase.

**Worked Example:**

Two triangles with sides 6,8,10 are congruent (both are scaled 3-4-5 right triangles). You can rotate and translate one onto the other; all sides and angles match.

## Corresponding Parts

<!-- section: corresponding-parts -->

When we write △ABC ≅ △DEF we mean angle A corresponds to angle D, side AB to DE, etc. The order of vertices encodes the correspondence.

Corresponding parts (CPCTC: corresponding parts of congruent triangles are congruent) are equal.

**ELI5**

- The labels tell you which corner goes with which corner. If you wrote the vertices in the wrong order, the correspondence would be wrong even if the numbers match.
- Once you know the triangles are congruent, every matching piece (sides, angles) must be equal.

**Worked Example:**

△ABC ≅ △DEF with correspondence A-D, B-E, C-F. Then ∠A = ∠D, AB = DE, BC = EF, etc. If we had written △ABC ≅ △DFE the correspondence would be different and the equalities would not hold.

## Criteria for Triangles

<!-- section: criteria-summary -->

The five standard triangle congruence criteria (SSS, SAS, ASA, AAS, HL) are shortcuts that guarantee an isometry exists without constructing it explicitly.

Each criterion is proved from the axioms (often using superposition or auxiliary constructions).

**ELI5**

- Instead of moving the whole triangle, we have quick "tests" using only 3 pieces of information that are enough to guarantee the triangles are identical twins.
- HL is special for right triangles (hypotenuse-leg).

**Worked Example:**

Given two right triangles with equal hypotenuses and one equal leg, they are congruent by HL. Proof reduces to SSS after constructing the other leg or using coordinates.

## Common Mistakes

- **Writing the correspondence in the wrong vertex order**: △ABC ≅ △DEF is not the same as △ABC ≅ △DFE.
- **Assuming SSS, SAS etc. work for any polygons**: They are triangle-specific (for quadrilaterals you need more information).
- **Confusing congruence with similarity**: Congruence requires equal size; similarity allows scaling.
- **Forgetting reflections**: In some contexts (oriented congruence) reflections are disallowed; usually in school geometry reflections are allowed.
