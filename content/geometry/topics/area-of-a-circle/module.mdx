# Area of a circle

The area of a circle of radius r is A = \pi r^{2}.

This formula can be derived by dividing the circle into sectors and rearranging them into a rectangle-like shape whose dimensions involve \pi r and r.

## Derivation by Sectors

<!-- section: derivation-sectors -->

Divide the circle into n thin sectors, each with angle 360°/n. Each sector has arc length (2\pi r)/n and 'height' r.

Rearranged, the sectors form a shape that approaches a rectangle of width \pi r and height r as n \to \infty. Area = \pi r \times r = \pi r^{2}.

**ELI5**

- Cut the pizza into many skinny slices and rearrange them into a rectangle that is half the circumference wide and radius tall.
- The wiggly edges become straight in the limit, giving the clean rectangle area.

**Worked Example:**

For r=1, limit area \pi. For r=5, A = 25\pi. Using 8 sectors gives a rough approximation; more sectors get closer to the true area.

## Other Derivations

<!-- section: other-derivations -->

Using integral calculus: area = \int_{-r}^r 2\sqrt{r^2 - x^2} dx = \pi r^{2} (area under semicircle).

Using Green's theorem or polar coordinates also yields the formula.

**ELI5**

- You can add up the areas of infinitely many thin rings or use trig to integrate the circle equation.
- All methods agree on \pi r^{2}.

**Worked Example:**

In polar: area = (1/2) \int_0^{2\pi} r^2 d\theta = (1/2) r^2 * 2\pi = \pi r^{2}.

## Applications and Scaling

<!-- section: applications-scaling -->

Area scales with the square of the linear dimensions: if you double the radius, area quadruples.

Used in physics (pressure on a circular piston, flux through circular aperture), probability (normal distribution in 2D), and design.

**ELI5**

- A circle twice as wide has four times as much 'stuff' inside (area).
- This quadratic scaling is why large pizzas are better value per crust (less crust per area).

**Worked Example:**

Circle r=1, A=\pi. Circle r=2, A=4\pi. Circle r=10, A=100\pi. Scaling factor k in radius gives k^{2} in area.

## Common Mistakes

- **Using 2\pi r for area**: That is circumference. Area is \pi r^{2}.
- **Forgetting the square**: \pi r is not area.
- **Using diameter instead of radius without adjusting**: A = (\pi/4) d^{2} is correct but easy to forget the 1/4.
- **Confusing with sphere surface/volume**: Sphere surface 4\pi r^{2}, volume (4/3)\pi r^{3}.
