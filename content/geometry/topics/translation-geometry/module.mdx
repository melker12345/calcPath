# Translation (geometry)

A translation is an isometry that slides every point of the plane the same distance in the same direction.

It is determined by a vector: every point P maps to P + v.

## Definition and Properties

<!-- section: definition-props -->

Translation by vector v: T(P) = P + v.

Preserves orientation and all distances and angles. No fixed points unless v = 0.

The composition of two translations is a translation by the sum of vectors.

**ELI5**

- Imagine the whole plane is a sheet of paper; you slide it without rotating or flipping. Every dot moves exactly the same way.
- Nothing stays in place (unless the slide is zero); the whole figure just relocates.

**Worked Example:**

Translate by (3, -1): (0,0) \to (3,-1), (1,4) \to (4,3), the segment between them moves rigidly to the image segment.

## Vector and Coordinate Form

<!-- section: vector-coord -->

In coordinates: (x, y) \to (x + a, y + b) for vector <a, b>.

In vectors: T(P) = P + v.

The inverse is translation by -v.

**ELI5**

- Just add the same numbers to every x and every y.
- To undo, subtract the same numbers.

**Worked Example:**

Point (2,5) translated by < -1, 3 > becomes (1,8). To go back, add <1, -3>.

## Translations in Congruence and Symmetry

<!-- section: congruence-symmetry -->

Translations are used to prove SSS and other criteria by superposition.

In wallpaper groups and frieze patterns, translations generate periodic symmetry.

**ELI5**

- To show two triangles are congruent by SSS, you can slide one so that one side coincides with the corresponding side of the other; the rest follows.
- Repeating a translation creates an infinite row of identical copies (like a picket fence pattern).

**Worked Example:**

To map △ABC to △DEF with AB=DE etc by SSS, first translate so A goes to D and the direction of AB aligns with DE (possible by choosing the translation vector DE - AB or appropriate). Then the third vertex follows.

## Common Mistakes

- **Thinking translations have a 'center'**: Unlike rotations, translations have no fixed point (unless trivial).
- **Confusing with rotation or glide**: Translation has no turning and no flip.
- **Forgetting that composition of translations adds vectors**: T_v ∘ T_w = T_{v+w}.
- **Assuming the image of a line under translation is the same line**: The image is a parallel line (or the same if v parallel to the line and the line is invariant, but generally parallel).
