# Cartesian coordinate system

The Cartesian coordinate system assigns to each point in the plane an ordered pair of real numbers (x, y) relative to two perpendicular axes intersecting at the origin.

It allows geometry to be translated into algebra and is the foundation of analytic geometry, calculus, and computer graphics.

## Axes, Origin, and Coordinates

<!-- section: axes-origin -->

Two perpendicular lines (x-axis horizontal, y-axis vertical) cross at the origin (0,0). The x-coordinate is the signed distance to the y-axis; y-coordinate to the x-axis.

Quadrants: I (+,+), II (−,+), III (−,−), IV (+,−).

**ELI5**

- The grid is like a city map with numbered streets (x) and avenues (y). The origin is the downtown crossing.
- Positive x is right, positive y is up; signs tell you which "neighborhood" (quadrant) the point is in.

**Worked Example:**

Point (3,−4) is in quadrant IV: 3 units right of y-axis, 4 units below x-axis. Point (−2,5) is in quadrant II.

## Distance, Midpoint, and Slope from Coordinates

<!-- section: distance-mid-slope -->

Distance between (x1,y1) and (x2,y2): \sqrt{(x2-x1)^2 + (y2-y1)^2} (Pythagorean).

Midpoint: ((x1+x2)/2, (y1+y2)/2).

Slope m = (y2-y1)/(x2-x1) (for x2 \neq x1).

**ELI5**

- Distance is the straight-line "as the crow flies" using the grid legs as a right triangle.
- Midpoint is the average address. Slope is how many y-units you rise per x-unit you run (steepness).

**Worked Example:**

Points (1,2) and (4,6). Distance \sqrt{3^2 + 4^2} = 5. Midpoint (2.5, 4). Slope = (6-2)/(4-1) = 4/3.

## Lines and Circles in Coordinates

<!-- section: lines-circles -->

Line: y = mx + b or ax + by + c = 0.

Circle: (x-h)^2 + (y-k)^2 = r^2 (center (h,k), radius r).

These equations turn geometric questions into algebraic equations that can be solved.

**ELI5**

- An equation is a rule that every point on the figure obeys. Plug in the coordinates; if true, the point is on the figure.
- Solving two equations finds intersection points (where two figures cross).

**Worked Example:**

Line y = 2x + 1 and circle x^2 + y^2 = 5. Substitute: x^2 + (2x+1)^2 = 5 \to 5x^2 + 4x -4 =0. Solve quadratic for intersection x-values, then y.

## Common Mistakes

- **Swapping x and y**: (3,4) is different from (4,3); x is always first (horizontal).
- **Forgetting signs when plotting**: Negative coordinates go left or down.
- **Assuming slope is always defined**: Vertical lines have undefined slope (x = constant).
- **Mixing distance formula with midpoint formula**: One has square root and squares; the other is just averages.
