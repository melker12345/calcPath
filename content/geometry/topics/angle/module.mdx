# Angle

An angle is the figure formed by two rays (or line segments) that share a common endpoint called the vertex. Angles measure the amount of rotation or "opening" between the two sides.

Angles are fundamental to trigonometry, navigation, architecture, and almost every geometric theorem involving triangles or circles.

## Measuring Angles

<!-- section: measuring -->

The size of an angle is measured in degrees (360° in a full turn) or radians (2π radians in a full turn). A right angle is 90° or π/2 radians. An acute angle is less than 90°; obtuse between 90° and 180°; reflex greater than 180°.

**ELI5**

- Imagine two clock hands. The angle tells you how far one hand has turned from the other. A right angle is a perfect corner like the corner of a piece of paper.
- Degrees are like slicing a pizza into 360 tiny equal slices; radians use the radius as the "slice width" around the circle.

**Worked Example:**

Convert 45° to radians: 45 × (π/180) = π/4 radians.

Convert π/3 radians to degrees: (π/3) × (180/π) = 60°.

These are the angles in an equilateral triangle (60°) and in an isosceles right triangle (45°).

## Types of Angles and Relations

<!-- section: types-relations -->

Adjacent angles share a side. Vertical angles are opposite when two lines cross and are always equal. Complementary angles add to 90°; supplementary add to 180°.

When a transversal crosses parallel lines, corresponding angles and alternate interior angles are equal (uses parallel postulate).

**ELI5**

- Vertical angles are the "X" pairs that look the same when lines cross.
- If two angles make a perfect corner together they are complementary (like 30° and 60°).

**Worked Example:**

Two lines cross forming angles of 35° and 145° adjacent. The vertical angle to the 35° is also 35°. The adjacent 145° is supplementary to 35° (35+145=180).

## Angles in Triangles and Polygons

<!-- section: polygons -->

The sum of interior angles in any triangle is 180°. For an n-sided polygon the sum is (n−2)×180°. An exterior angle equals the sum of the two remote interior angles.

**ELI5**

- Tear the corners off a paper triangle and rearrange them; they form a straight line (180°).
- A square has four 90° corners: 4×90 = 360° = (4−2)×180.

**Worked Example:**

In △ABC, ∠A = 40°, ∠B = 60°. Then ∠C = 180° − 40° − 60° = 80°. An exterior angle at C equals 40° + 60° = 100°.

## Common Mistakes

- **Measuring the reflex angle instead of the interior one**: Always check which region the angle is in; diagrams may be ambiguous.
- **Forgetting that vertical angles are equal**: Many students think adjacent angles are equal.
- **Using 360° for triangle sum**: That is the sum around a point or for a quadrilateral.
- **Confusing radians and degrees in calculations**: Always convert when mixing trig functions that expect radians in some software.
