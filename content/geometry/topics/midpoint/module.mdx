# Midpoint

The midpoint of a line segment is the point that divides the segment into two equal parts. It is the average of the endpoints.

In coordinate geometry the midpoint of (x1,y1) and (x2,y2) is ((x1+x2)/2, (y1+y2)/2).

## Definition and Coordinate Formula

<!-- section: definition-formula -->

The midpoint M of AB is the unique point on AB such that AM = MB.

In vectors, M = (A + B)/2.

In coordinates, average the corresponding components.

**ELI5**

- The midpoint is the exact middle of the segment; if you fold the segment in half the crease is at the midpoint.
- On a map, the midpoint is the average address of the two ends.

**Worked Example:**

Endpoints (2,3) and (8,11). Midpoint x = (2+8)/2 = 5, y = (3+11)/2 = 7. So (5,7). Distance from each end is the same.

## Properties and Uses

<!-- section: properties-uses -->

The midpoint is the center of symmetry of the segment. The line segment joining midpoints of two sides of a triangle is parallel to the third and half as long (midline theorem).

Midpoints are used in the definition of medians, in vector geometry, and in computer graphics for subdivision.

**ELI5**

- Connecting the midpoints of a triangle gives a smaller triangle similar and parallel-sided (Varignon's for quads too).
- It is the balance point if the segment had uniform mass.

**Worked Example:**

In △ABC the segment joining midpoints of AB and AC is parallel to BC and half length (midline or midpoint theorem). This is a special case of similar triangles or vector average.

## Common Mistakes

- **Using the distance formula instead of averaging**: Midpoint is not the distance; it is the coordinate average.
- **Forgetting to average both coordinates**: You must do x and y separately.
- **Thinking the midpoint is always the origin**: Only if the segment is centered at 0.
- **Confusing with median**: The median is the segment from vertex to midpoint of opposite side; the midpoint is the end point of the median on the side.
