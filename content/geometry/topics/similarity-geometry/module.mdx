# Similarity (geometry)

Two figures are similar if they have the same shape but not necessarily the same size. For triangles this means corresponding angles are equal and corresponding sides are proportional.

Similarity is the foundation of scale drawings, maps, fractals, and much of trigonometry.

## Definition for Triangles

<!-- section: definition-triangles -->

△ABC ~ △DEF if ∠A = ∠D, ∠B = ∠E, ∠C = ∠F and the ratios AB/DE = BC/EF = CA/FD = k (the scale factor).

The order of vertices indicates the correspondence.

**ELI5**

- Similar triangles are like photographs of the same scene taken from different distances: angles stay the same, but everything gets bigger or smaller together.
- The sides "scale" by the same factor; the shape is identical.

**Worked Example:**

△ABC has angles 40°, 60°, 80° and sides opposite 3, 4.2, 4.9 (approx). △DEF has the same angles and sides opposite 6, 8.4, 9.8. Scale factor k=2; the triangles are similar.

## AA, SAS, and SSS Similarity Criteria

<!-- section: criteria -->

Because of the 180° sum, two equal angles (AA) suffice for similarity of triangles. There are also SAS similarity (two sides proportional with included angle equal) and SSS similarity (all three sides proportional).

**ELI5**

- AA is the easiest: if two angles match, the third must, so shape matches.
- SSS similarity says "all sides in the same proportions" — like a photocopy at a different zoom.

**Worked Example:**

Two triangles have sides 4,5,6 and 8,10,12. All ratios = 2, so SSS similarity; all angles equal even without measuring them.

## Applications

<!-- section: applications -->

Similar triangles let us solve for unknown lengths using proportions. They justify the basic trigonometric ratios (sin, cos, tan are constant for a given angle across all similar right triangles). Used in shadow problems, map scaling, and perspective drawing.

**ELI5**

- If you know a tree casts a 10 ft shadow when a 6 ft person casts a 4 ft shadow, the triangles are similar and you can set up 6/4 = h/10 to find the tree height h=15 ft.
- Ratios replace measuring everything directly.

**Worked Example:**

A 1.7 m person stands 3 m from a lamppost and casts a 0.85 m shadow. The lamppost casts a 4.25 m shadow. Similar triangles: 1.7 / 0.85 = h / 4.25 ⇒ h = 8.5 m.

## Common Mistakes

- **Confusing similarity with congruence**: Similar triangles can be different sizes; congruent ones must be the same size.
- **Wrong correspondence**: The angles that are equal must be matched correctly when writing the proportion.
- **Assuming all triangles with one equal angle are similar**: Need two angles (or sides in proportion with angle).
- **Forgetting that similarity preserves angles but scales areas by k^{2}**: Linear dimensions scale by k, areas by k^{2}.
