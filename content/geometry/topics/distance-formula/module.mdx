# Distance formula

The distance formula gives the Euclidean distance between two points in the plane: d = \sqrt{(x_2 - x_1)^2 + (y_2 - y_1)^2}.

It is a direct application of the Pythagorean theorem to the differences in coordinates.

## Derivation

<!-- section: derivation -->

Given points P1(x1,y1), P2(x2,y2), form the right triangle with legs |x2-x1| (horizontal) and |y2-y1| (vertical). By Pythagorean, hypotenuse (straight-line distance) is the square root of sum of squares of legs.

**ELI5**

- Draw a horizontal and vertical line between the two points; they form the legs of a right triangle. The straight line between the points is the hypotenuse.
- Square, add, square-root — the same 3-4-5 idea for any numbers.

**Worked Example:**

(0,0) to (3,4): legs 3 and 4, d = \sqrt{9+16} = 5.

(1,2) to (4,6): \Delta x=3, \Delta y=4, same 5.

## In 3D and Higher

<!-- section: higher-d -->

In 3D: \sqrt{(x2-x1)^2 + (y2-y1)^2 + (z2-z1)^2}.

The formula generalizes to any dimension: the Euclidean norm of the difference vector.

**ELI5**

- In 3D you just add the squared difference in the third direction (up/down) before taking square root.
- It is the "ordinary" straight-line distance we experience in our space.

**Worked Example:**

(0,0,0) to (1,2,2): d = \sqrt{1+4+4} = 3.

## Applications

<!-- section: applications-dist -->

Used everywhere: length of vectors, collision detection, GPS (great-circle is different but Euclidean is local approximation), computer graphics, physics (displacement magnitude).

**ELI5**

- Any time you want "how far apart are these two spots on the map or in the game world," you use the distance formula (or its 3D version).
- It tells you the length of the arrow from one point to the other.

**Worked Example:**

Two game characters at (10,20) and (13,24). Distance = \sqrt{3^2 + 4^2} = 5 units. If attack range is 4, they are just out of range.

## Common Mistakes

- **Forgetting the square root**: The sum of squares is not the distance; you must take the root to get length.
- **Mixing up which is x and y**: Order inside the deltas does not matter (squared), but you must subtract corresponding coordinates.
- **Using Manhattan distance (|dx| + |dy|) by mistake**: That is grid-path distance, not straight-line.
- **Applying in non-Euclidean spaces without adjustment**: On a sphere or in taxicab geometry the formula is different.
