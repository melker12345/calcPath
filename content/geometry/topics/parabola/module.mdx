# Parabola

A parabola is the set of points in the plane equidistant from a fixed point (focus) and a fixed line (directrix).

It is one of the conic sections and the graph of a quadratic function y = a x^{2} + b x + c.

## Definition and Standard Forms

<!-- section: definition-forms -->

Standard vertical: (x - h)^{2} = 4p (y - k), focus (h, k+p), directrix y = k-p.

Standard horizontal: (y - k)^{2} = 4p (x - h).

Vertex at (h,k); p is the focal length.

**ELI5**

- Every point on the parabola is exactly as far from the focus "magnet" as from the directrix "wall."
- The vertex is the "bottom" (or side) where it turns around.

**Worked Example:**

y = (1/4) x^{2} : 4p = 1, p=1/4. Focus (0,1/4), directrix y=-1/4. Point (0,0) on parabola: distance to focus 1/4, to directrix 1/4.

## Reflective Property and Applications

<!-- section: reflective-apps -->

Rays parallel to the axis reflect off the parabola and pass through the focus (and vice versa).

Used in satellite dishes, headlights, telescopes, and the paths of projectiles (under constant gravity, ignoring air resistance).

**ELI5**

- A parabolic mirror sends all parallel incoming rays (from a distant star or satellite) to one point (the focus), where you put your receiver.
- Headlights do the reverse: light at the focus bounces into a parallel beam.

**Worked Example:**

Satellite dish shaped as paraboloid of revolution. Signal from satellite (essentially parallel rays) hits the dish and reflects to the feed horn at the focus. All paths have equal length, signals arrive in phase.

## Vertex Form and Completing the Square

<!-- section: vertex-form -->

y = a (x - h)^{2} + k is vertex form. a determines width and direction (positive up).

Completing the square on y = a x ^{2} + b x + c converts to vertex form.

**ELI5**

- Vertex form shows the 'turning point' (h,k) and how 'wide' (a) the parabola opens directly.
- Completing the square is the algebra trick that reveals the vertex without graphing.

**Worked Example:**

y = 2x^{2} + 8x + 5 = 2(x+2) ^{2} - 3. Vertex (-2, -3), opens upward, narrower than y=x^{2}.

## Common Mistakes

- **Confusing focus/directrix with vertex**: The vertex is midway between focus and directrix.
- **Mixing vertical and horizontal parabolas**: y = x^{2} is vertical (opens up/down); x = y^{2} is horizontal.
- **Forgetting the 4p in the standard form**: The coefficient is 4p, not p.
- **Thinking all quadratic graphs are parabolas in the geometric sense**: The geometric definition (focus/directrix) is equivalent to the algebraic quadratic only after rotation/translation in the plane.
