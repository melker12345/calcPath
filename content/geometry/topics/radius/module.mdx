# Radius

The radius of a circle or sphere is the distance from its center to any point on its circumference (or surface). It is usually denoted r and is the fundamental length parameter of the circle.

All radii of a given circle are equal, and the radius determines every other measurement of the circle.

## Definition and Properties

<!-- section: definition-properties -->

The radius r is half the diameter. The set of points at distance r from center O is the circle. Any segment from center to circumference is a radius and has length r.

Because all radii are equal, triangles formed by two radii and a chord are isosceles.

**ELI5**

- The radius is the "spoke" length from the axle (center) to the rim. Every spoke is the same.
- Once you fix r, the whole circle is fixed; you cannot have a bigger or smaller circle with the same r.

**Worked Example:**

Center at (2,3), radius 4. The point (2+4,3)=(6,3) is on the circle because distance =4. The point (2,3+4)=(2,7) is also on the circle. Any point at distance exactly 4 from (2,3) lies on it.

## Radius in Formulas

<!-- section: formulas -->

Circumference C = 2πr. Area A = πr^{2}. In a unit circle r=1, many trig identities simplify.

The length of a chord subtending central angle 2\theta is 2r sin\theta.

**ELI5**

- Circumference is "unrolled" length around the circle: like the string you wrap exactly once.
- Area is "how much carpet" inside the circle; it grows with the square of the radius (bigger r means much more area).

**Worked Example:**

r = 5. C = 2\pi*5 = 10\pi \approx 31.4. A = \pi*25 \approx 78.5. Chord for 60° central: 2*5*sin(30°) = 5.

## Radius vs Other Lengths

<!-- section: vs-other -->

The radius is the smallest distance from center to boundary. Apothem of a regular polygon is the radius of the inscribed circle. In 3D the radius of a sphere is analogous.

**ELI5**

- The radius is the shortest "ruler" from middle to edge. Any other line from center to edge is also a radius, same length.
- For a square, the radius of the inscribed circle touches the middle of each side (apothem); the circumradius goes to the corners.

**Worked Example:**

For a regular hexagon with side length s, the radius (distance from center to vertex) equals s. This makes every central triangle equilateral.

## Common Mistakes

- **Using diameter when radius is needed in formulas**: Many formulas (area, C=2\pi r) explicitly use r; doubling by mistake is common.
- **Thinking radius can vary around the circle**: In a true circle it cannot; varying distance means it is not a circle (ellipse, etc.).
- **Confusing radius of circle with radius of curvature**: For more complex curves the local radius of curvature can change.
- **Forgetting r=0 is a degenerate point-circle**: A circle of radius 0 is just its center point.
