# Algebraic Integers

An algebraic integer is a complex number that is a root of a monic polynomial with integer coefficients.

Examples: all ordinary integers Z, sqrt(2), (1+sqrt(-3))/2, roots of x^2 - x -1=0 (golden ratio), etc.

The algebraic integers form a ring (sum and product of algebraic integers are algebraic integers).

In a number field, the algebraic integers inside it form the ring O_K.

They generalize the notion of "integer" beyond Z, and their arithmetic is richer (and harder).

## Definition and Basic Properties

<!-- section: alg-int-def -->

α algebraic integer if there is monic f(x) = x^n + a_{n-1} x^{n-1} + ... + a0 ∈ Z[x] with f(α)=0.

Equiv, the minimal polynomial over Q is monic with Z coeffs.

The set of all algebraic integers is a ring: if α,β alg int, then Z[α,β] is finitely generated as Z-module (basis products of powers), hence α+β, αβ integral over Z.

**ELI5**

- Like integers, but allowed to satisfy higher degree monic eqs like x^2=2 or x^2 +x +1=0.
- Closed under + and *, so you can add and multiply them and stay "integral".

**Worked Example:**

**Golden ratio φ=(1+sqrt5)/2 satisfies x^2 -x -1=0 monic Z coeffs, hence alg integer. φ+1 = (3+sqrt5)/2 also, etc.**

## Algebraic Numbers vs Integers

<!-- section: alg-int-vs-numbers -->

An algebraic number is root of any nonzero poly with Q coeffs (or Z).

The algebraic numbers form a field (the algebraic closure of Q in C).

Algebraic integers are the "integers" among them: those with monic min poly over Z.

The quotient field of the alg integers is the field of alg numbers.

**ELI5**

- Algebraic numbers are "fractions" of alg integers in some sense (every alg number is alg int divided by ordinary int).
- Z is to Q as alg integers to alg numbers.

**Worked Example:**

**1/2 is algebraic number (root of 2x-1=0), but not alg integer (min poly 2x-1 not monic? Min poly x-1/2, leading 1 but coeffs not all Z; or clear: suppose monic Z poly, then by rational root or Gauss would imply 1/2 integer, contradiction).**

## Rings of Algebraic Integers in Extensions

<!-- section: alg-int-rings -->

If K/Q finite, the alg integers in K are exactly O_K the integral closure.

O_K is Z-free of rank [K:Q], and is the "right" ring to do arithmetic in K.

For quadratic fields explicit as above.

**ELI5**

- When you adjoin an algebraic number to Q, the "integers" in the new field are all the alg integers that live there (not just polynomials in the adjoined element).
- It may require a bigger Z-basis than the obvious powers.

**Worked Example:**

**Q(sqrt(1/2)) = Q(sqrt2), but  sqrt(1/2)= sqrt2 /2 is alg number but the integers are still those of Q(sqrt2), which include sqrt2 (alg int) but not necessarily halves unless in the basis.**

## Minimal Polynomial and Characteristic Polynomial

<!-- section: alg-int-minpoly-charpoly -->

If α alg int of degree d, its min poly m(x) monic in Z[x].

If K=Q(α), the char poly of multiplication-by-α on K as Q-vector space is m(x)^ {[K:Q(α)] ? No for power basis.

In general for element in O_K, its char poly over Q is monic in Z[x], and the constant term ± norm, trace etc are in Z.

**ELI5**

- The min poly tells the "equation" α satisfies, with integer coeffs monic.
- The char poly of the linear map "multiply by α" on the vector space K gives the norm (det) and trace (sum eigenvalues) which are integers for alg int α.

**Worked Example:**

**For α=sqrt2 in Q(sqrt2), char poly of mult by α is x^2 -2 (the min poly). Norm of α is - const = -(-2)? Char poly x^2 - tr x + norm = x^2 -0x -2? Norm of sqrt2 is -2? N( a +b sqrt2)=a^2 -2 b^2, N(sqrt2)=-2. Yes, and -(-2) or sign convention in char poly det(xI - m_α).**

## Common Mistakes

- **Every algebraic number is an algebraic integer**: No; 1/2 is alg number not integer.
- **The ring of all alg integers is a UFD or PID**: No, it is not even Noetherian; it is a Bezout domain or something but factorization is wild (infinitely many primes etc).
- **Z[sqrt(-3)] is the full ring of integers of Q(sqrt(-3))**: No, for d=-3=1 mod4, O_K = Z[ω] the Eisenstein, larger, includes ( -1 + sqrt(-3))/2.
- **Min poly always has integer coeffs for alg numbers**: For alg numbers min poly has rational coeffs; for alg int it can be taken monic Z.
- **α alg int implies 1/α alg int**: No (unless unit).
