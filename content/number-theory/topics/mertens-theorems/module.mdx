# Mertens' Theorems

Mertens' theorems are three asymptotic estimates involving products and sums over primes:

1. sum_{p≤x} 1/p ~ log log x

2. ∏_{p≤x} (1 - 1/p) ~ e^{-γ} / log x   (γ Euler's constant)

3. sum_{p≤x} log p / p ~ log x

They are elementary consequences of the prime number theorem (or Chebyshev bounds suffice for some), and are useful in sieve theory, probabilistic number theory, and estimates for φ(n), the primorial, etc.

Named after Franz Mertens (1897).

## The Three Theorems

<!-- section: mertens-three -->

Mertens' theorems (1897):

(1) \sum_{p \le x} \frac{1}{p} \sim \log\log x

(2) \prod_{p \le x} \left(1 - \frac{1}{p}\right) \sim \frac{e^{-\gamma}}{\log x}

(3) \sum_{p \le x} \frac{\log p}{p} \sim \log x

Where γ ≈0.57721 is the Euler-Mascheroni constant (lim (H_n - log n)).

**ELI5**

- The sum of 1/p up to x grows like the log of the log (very slowly).
- The product over (1-1/p) is the "probability" a random number is not divisible by any p≤x, i.e. either 1 or has all prime factors >x; it decays like 1/log x.
- The third is the "weighted" sum with log p /p ~ log x, related to the integral of the PNT.

**Worked Example:**

**For x=10, primes 2,3,5,7. sum 1/p ≈ 1/2+1/3+1/5+1/7≈ 0.5+0.333+0.2+0.143≈1.176**

log log 10 ≈ log 2.3 ≈0.83, rough.

Product (1-1/2)(1-1/3)(1-1/5)(1-1/7)≈0.5*0.666*0.8*0.857≈0.228

e^{-γ}/log10 ≈ 0.561 / 2.3 ≈0.244, close.

## Proofs from PNT or Chebyshev

<!-- section: mertens-proofs -->

From PNT ψ(x)~x one deduces sum_{p≤x} log p /p ~ log x by partial summation or Stieltjes integral.

Then sum 1/p = integral d( sum logp /p ) / log p or partial summation: sum_{p≤x} 1/p = (sum logp /p )/log x + integral (sum logp/p / t (log t)^2 ) dt ~ log x / log x + integral dt / (t log t) ~ log log x.

For the product, take log of the product = sum log(1-1/p) ~ - sum 1/p - (1/2 sum 1/p^2 +...) ~ - log log x + const (the const from the Euler product for 1/ζ or Mertens constant involving γ).

The e^{-γ} comes from the definition of γ via the harmonic and the zeta or the product.

Chebyshev bounds suffice for the ~ with unspecified constants; PNT gives the precise asymptotic with the e^{-γ}.

**ELI5**

- Partial summation turns sums involving 1/p into integrals of the prime counting or Chebyshev function.
- The product log is sum log(1-1/p) ~ -sum 1/p, and the constant in the asymptotic is fixed by matching with the harmonic number or zeta(1+) behavior.

**Worked Example:**

**The Mertens constant B1 = γ + sum_p [log(1-1/p) +1/p ] ≈ 0.261497... appears in the precise product ~ e^{-γ} / log x * (1 + O(1/log x)).**

## Applications

<!-- section: mertens-applications -->

- Estimates for φ(n)/n = ∏_{p|n} (1-1/p) ~ e^{-γ} / log log n or for primorial n# the product of p≤n.

- In sieve of Eratosthenes, the number of integers ≤x not divisible by p≤y is ~ x ∏ (1-1/p) ~ x e^{-γ} / log y.

- Probabilistic models of integers (the "probability" an integer is prime is 1/log , square-free ∏ (1-1/p^2)=1/ζ(2) etc).

- In the proof of infinitude of primes in AP or other, the divergence of sum 1/p is used.

**ELI5**

- The product ∏ (1-1/p) tells the "density" of integers free of small prime factors (smooth or rough numbers).
- It appears everywhere in asymptotic sieve estimates and the "normal order" of the number of distinct prime factors ω(n) ~ log log n.

**Worked Example:**

**The probability a random integer near x is not divisible by any prime ≤ log x is about e^{-γ} / log log x , very small, but positive.**

## Relation to Zeta and Euler Product

<!-- section: mertens-zeta-relation -->

Taking log ζ(s) ~ sum 1/p^s for s→1+, and the Laurent ζ(s) ~ 1/(s-1), log ~ -log(s-1).

On the other hand sum 1/p^s ~ integral or by PNT relates to log log .

The constant γ appears from the Weierstrass product for 1/Γ or the definition of γ = -Γ'(1).

**ELI5**

- The pole of zeta at 1 "generates" the divergence of sum 1/p , and the rate gives log log .
- The Euler-Mascheroni constant bridges the harmonic series and the log, and appears in the Mertens product via the same limit.

**Worked Example:**

**From Mertens (2), taking reciprocal, the harmonic product over p≤x p/(p-1) ~ (e^γ log x), which is consistent with the divergence of sum 1/p ~ log log x.**

## Common Mistakes

- **Mertens theorems are equivalent to PNT**: The third is equivalent to PNT; the first and second follow from weaker Chebyshev bounds (with constants).
- **The product includes p=2 always**: Yes.
- **γ is the Euler constant from zeta(3) or other**: No, the Mascheroni γ from H_n - log n.
- **The product ∏ (1-1/p) over all p converges to 0**: Yes, since sum 1/p diverges, the infinite product ∏ (1-1/p) =0.
- **Applies to all integers**: The estimates are asymptotic as x→∞; for small x one checks directly.
